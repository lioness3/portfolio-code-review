The Adventure from Coast to Coast

About Me

Hello, my name is Joann Carter. I prefer to go by Jo or Jojo. I am an only child and my parents have always encouraged me to be kind, honest and hardworking.I have had a difficult time figuring out what I want to do with the rest of my life. I consider myself an adventurous person because I thrive on being spontaneous. For example, I recently moved to the West Coast.

Besides, traveling and random summer adventures, I have lived in Southern NH my entire life. Being spontataneous has its advantages because of the many opportunities to acquire new experiences. I have also tried many different careers. Such as:

  • Security Sales Manager
  • Personal Shopper
  • Automotive Repair Estimator
  • Mixologist
  • Manicurist

As you can see, I have tried a wide variety of jobs. My most long lived career was as a manicurist at a fancy salon in NH. I was there for 9 years. It was difficult trying to prove my income with commission based jobs and, after a year of house hunting, I decided to find a studio apartemnt to rent until the housing market crashed.

On memorial day of 2019, my cousin from the West Coast came to visit. Hearing my frustration, he suggested a coding bootcamp for a fast track career to stabilitize my income. Even though he was pretty convincing, I declined because I was signing a leasing for a studio the very next day. Turns out the landlord chose a tenant with no pets so I took that as a sign to go live near my cousin and attend Epicodus.

The Adventure to the West Coast

I left NH with my cat on Thursday October 3rd 2019 and I arrived in Vancouver, WA on Saturday October 12th 2019.

These are the stops I made along the way:

  1. Niagra Falls, NY
  2. Toledo, OH
  3. Omaha, NE
  4. Denver, CO
  5. Boise, ID
  6. Vancouver, WA
My favorite stop was Niagra Falls

Arriving in Washington

I made it to Vancouver with little time to spare. Unpacking and preparing for class was a challenge. This kaos gave me no time to be nervous for my first day. Turns out, I really enjoy coding! I am pleased with my progress since my first few assignments.
